INTELLIGENCE INCIDENT REPORT

Incident Date: 14 February 2019

Location:
Lethpora, Pulwama District, Jammu and Kashmir, India

Incident Summary:
On 14 February 2019, a convoy of the Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) travelling along National Highway 44 near Lethpora in Pulwama district was attacked by a suicide bomber using an explosives-laden vehicle. The explosion resulted in significant damage to multiple convoy vehicles and caused heavy casualties among security personnel.

Preliminary investigation indicated that the attack was claimed by the terrorist organization Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM). Security agencies immediately launched search operations across nearby villages while forensic teams collected evidence from the blast site.

Attack Type:
Suicide Bombing

Weapon Used:
Vehicle-Borne Improvised Explosive Device (VBIED)

Primary Target:
Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF) convoy

Responsible Group:
Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM)

Casualties:
Killed: 40
Injured: 35

Follow-up Actions:
The National Investigation Agency (NIA) initiated an investigation into the incident. Security forces increased surveillance across the region and additional checkpoints were established to prevent further attacks.
