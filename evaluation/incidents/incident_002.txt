On 26 November 2008, a series of coordinated terrorist attacks took place across Mumbai, India. Ten members of Lashkar-e-Taiba carried out 12 coordinated shooting and bombing attacks lasting four days. The attacks targeted the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Oberoi Trident Hotel, Leopold Cafe, Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus railway station, and Nariman House. A total of 166 people were killed and over 300 were injured. Nine of the attackers were killed, and the lone surviving gunman, Ajmal Kasab, was captured and later executed.
