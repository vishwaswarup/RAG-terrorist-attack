On 21 April 2019, Easter Sunday, a series of coordinated suicide bombings struck three churches and three luxury hotels in Sri Lanka. The attacks targeted St. Anthony's Church in Colombo, St. Sebastian's Church in Negombo, Zion Church in Batticaloa, the Shangri-La Hotel, Cinnamon Grand Hotel, and Kingsbury Hotel in Colombo. The National Thowheed Jamath carried out the attacks with support from the Islamic State. At least 269 people were killed and over 500 were injured in the blasts.
